VPN Setup

Connect with WireGuard before using the application

Warehouse access to the PDI Tool requires an active VPN connection. Your administrator will provision a personal VPN profile and share a config file and QR code by email. Use the steps below on your device.

Ask your administrator for your VPN profile if you have not received wireguard-vpn.conf and a QR image yet.

Choose your device

Mobile setup steps

  1. Install WireGuard

    Download WireGuard from the Google Play Store or App Store.

  2. Add your VPN tunnel

    Open WireGuard and add a tunnel using one of these options:

    • Import the config file from your administrator and choose Import from file or archive.
    • Scan the QR image from your administrator and choose Create from QR code.

  3. Connect to the warehouse VPN

    Enable the tunnel in WireGuard. Once connected, open the PDI Tool in your browser or install the Ops app and sign in.

Desktop setup steps

  1. Install WireGuard

    Download and install WireGuard from wireguard.com/install.

  2. Import your config file

    Save the .conf file from your administrator. In the WireGuard app, choose Import tunnel(s) from file and select the downloaded config.

  3. Activate the tunnel

    Turn on the tunnel to connect to the warehouse VPN, then open the PDI Tool in your browser and sign in.

Admin

Business oversight on the web dashboard

Admins monitor inspection performance, review operational data, and stay informed across the warehouses assigned to their account. This role uses the desktop dashboard — not the floor Ops mobile app.

Application
Web dashboard
Typical device
Desktop or laptop

Primary use cases

  • Executive analytics — Track inbound and outbound inspection trends, approvals, and rejections for your warehouse scope.
  • Inspection oversight — Browse overall, in-review, approved, and rejected inspection lists across inbound and outbound flows.
  • Master data visibility — Reference product categories, products, plants, and warehouses that underpin inspections on the floor.
  • Review notifications — Receive alerts when warehouse teams submit inspections that need managerial attention.

Warehouse Manager

Review and supervise warehouse inspections

Warehouse managers work in the Ops app on a phone or tablet. They approve or reject operator submissions, monitor team performance, and look up units by barcode — without starting new inbound or outbound inspections themselves.

Application
Warehouse Ops (mobile)
Typical device
Phone or tablet on the floor

Primary use cases

  • Inspection review — Open the review queue from Home and approve or reject inspections submitted by operators.
  • Team KPIs — Use the Inspections tab to review team metrics, change the reporting period, and drill into underlying records.
  • Barcode lookup — Search existing inspections by unit barcode to answer questions on the floor without creating new work.
  • Shift hygiene — Manage account settings, sign out at end of shift, and keep the device aligned with warehouse policy.

Warehouse Operator

Perform inbound and outbound inspections on the floor

Operators capture inspection work at the unit level — scanning barcodes, completing checklists, and submitting results for manager review. This is the hands-on role for day-to-day PDI activity in the warehouse.

Application
Warehouse Ops (mobile)
Typical device
Phone or handheld scanner

Primary use cases

  • New inspections — Start inbound or outbound inspections from Home or the New tab by scanning or entering the unit barcode.
  • Personal KPIs — Track your own inspection counts, approvals, and rejections over a selected reporting period.
  • Inspection lookup — Search by barcode to reopen or verify existing inbound or outbound records on the floor.
  • Device and session management — Keep your profile, notifications, and sign-out routine consistent across shifts.
